5 Ways to Lower Your Auto Insurance Rate in Mississippi

Most drivers overpay for auto insurance without realizing it. Here are five things you can do right now to bring your rate down — without sacrificing coverage.

  • Shop multiple carriers every 6–12 months — rates change constantly and loyalty rarely pays
  • Ask about bundling your auto with home or renters — most carriers give significant discounts
  • Raise your deductible if you have savings — going from $500 to $1,000 can cut your premium noticeably
  • Keep your policy active — even a short lapse flags you as higher risk and raises your rate
  • Call an independent agent — we compare 8+ companies at once so you don't have to

Liability vs. Full Coverage: Which One Do You Actually Need?

This is the question we get more than almost any other. The answer depends on your vehicle, your finances, and your situation — here's how to think about it.

  • Liability only covers damage you cause to others — your own car gets nothing if you're at fault
  • Full coverage adds collision (accidents) and comprehensive (theft, weather, fire)
  • If your car is worth less than $4,000, full coverage may cost more than the car is worth
  • If you have a car loan or lease, your lender requires full coverage — no exceptions
  • When in doubt, ask us — we'll run the numbers for your specific vehicle and budget

What Happens When Your Insurance Lapses — And How to Recover

Life happens. Payments get missed, policies cancel, and suddenly you're driving uninsured in Mississippi. Here's what you need to know and how to get back covered fast.

  • Mississippi requires minimum liability coverage — driving without it risks fines, license suspension, and worse
  • A lapse of even a few days can raise your next rate — the longer the lapse, the higher the risk flag
  • Not all carriers penalize lapses equally — independent agents can find the most lenient options
  • You can often get same-day coverage — call us and we'll get you back on the road today
  • Setting up autopay after reinstatement is the single best way to prevent it from happening again

Life & Burial

Burial Insurance: What Mississippi Families Should Know

Final expense insurance is one of the most overlooked but most important policies for families in Mississippi. Here's a plain-English breakdown of what it is and who needs it.

  • The average funeral in Mississippi costs $7,000–$12,000 — most families aren't prepared for that
  • Burial insurance is a small whole life policy — typically $5,000–$25,000 — that covers final expenses
  • Premiums are affordable and fixed — often $20–$60/month depending on age and coverage amount
  • Approval is simple — no medical exam required for most policies, just a few health questions
  • It's one of the greatest gifts you can give your family — peace of mind when they need it most

Business Owners
5 min read

What Every Mississippi Small Business Owner Needs to Know About Commercial Insurance

Running a business without the right insurance is one of the biggest financial risks you can take. Here's what coverage you actually need — and what you can skip.

  • General liability is the foundation — it covers injuries, property damage, and lawsuits on your premises
  • Commercial auto is separate from personal auto — using your personal vehicle for business can void your coverage
  • A business owners policy (BOP) bundles liability and property — often the most cost-effective option for small businesses
  • If you have employees, workers' comp may be required in Mississippi depending on your industry
  • The best time to review your commercial coverage is before something happens — not after
06
Home Insurance
4 min read

Home Insurance in Mississippi: What's Covered and What's Not

Mississippi homeowners face unique risks — from severe weather to flooding. Here's what your standard home policy covers and where you may have dangerous gaps.

  • Standard homeowners policies cover fire, wind, hail, theft, and liability — but NOT floods
  • Flood insurance is separate and must be purchased through the National Flood Insurance Program or private carriers
  • Mississippi's storm risk means your wind/hail deductible may be higher than you realize — read your policy
  • If you have a mobile or manufactured home, you need a specialty policy — standard homeowners won't cover it
  • Renters insurance is one of the most affordable policies available — often under $20/month for significant protection
